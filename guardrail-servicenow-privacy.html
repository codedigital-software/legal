Guardrail for ServiceNow — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 30 June 2026 · Provided by Codedigital.

Guardrail for ServiceNow is a browser extension that warns before destructive actions on ServiceNow production instances. It runs entirely on your device. It has no backend, no account, and no tracking — it does not send any data to us or to anyone else.

What we store on your device

  • Settings only — your instance classifications (which instances you mark as production or non-production), your guarded-action rules, and display preferences. Stored locally in the browser (chrome.storage) and synced across your own signed-in browser profile by the browser itself. We never receive it.

What we transmit

Nothing. The extension makes no network requests to any server we operate — there is no licence check, no analytics, and no telemetry. Your ServiceNow records, scripts, and business data are never read for collection and never leave your browser; the guardrail and banner operate purely on the page in front of you.

The “team version” link

The popup contains a link to an external waitlist form. It opens in a new tab only if you click it. Anything you choose to enter there is handled by that form’s provider under their privacy policy — not by this extension.

What we do NOT collect

  • No browsing history, page content, or analytics.
  • No personal or contact information.
  • No ServiceNow records, scripts, credentials, or session data.

Permissions

The extension requests only storage (to save your settings) and access to *.service-now.com (so it can show the environment banner and the confirmation dialog on those pages). It does not request cookie access, and it loads no remote code — all code is bundled in the package.

Data retention & your choices

Local settings remain until you remove the extension or clear its storage. Because nothing is stored on our side, there is nothing for us to delete. Questions: cowijin@protonmail.com.
